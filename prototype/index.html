The G.O.A.T. debate in sports

The greatest athelete of any sport has always been up for discussion. It was not until the late 2010s where people began to speak on who the G.O.A.T truly was. The G.O.A.T. is referred to as the greatest of all time. For women the term G.W.O.A.T. has been used to describe the greatestwoman of all time.

There are plenty of sports in which the G.O.A.T and G.W.O.A.T will be up for debate. The following will take a look at a few highly debated people. The topic is clearly opinion based with people throwing facts in the mix to strenghten their case. Nobody can be right or wrong in any case. The idea is kind of silly in terms of true importance. That does not mean it is not fun to discuss.The very opinionated Kadrian once said, The goat debate in a team based sport is just wrong

Basketball

In the team game of basketball there has been 5 guys to be called the goat as of 2014. After that the list has grown to an unreasonable number with mosy experts trying to keep the narrative at 3-4 people. The people played in different eras and had fastly different skillsets. The idea of winning, teammate, championships, likability, and other factors come into play when discussing these guys. The idea ruins the game for a lot of guys, but it does show the knowledge and experience of a lot of people when having the discussion. The following list will show some of the lighly talked about guys.

  • Magic Johnson, Stephen Curry, John Stockton
  • Michael Jordan, Kobe Bryant, Allen Iverson
  • LeBron James, Larry Bird, Kevin Durat
  • Kevin Garnett, Julius Erving, Giannis Antetokounmpo.
  • Bill Russell, Wilt Chamberlain, Kareem Abdul Jabbar, Shaq
The most common names that come up in the goat debate are Michael Jordan, LeBron James, and Kareem Abdul Jabbar, and Kobe Bryant with Stephen Curry making waves in the conversation. Sadly guys who maintained dominance like Tim Duncan are never spoken about.

Combat Sports

Weight Classes
Boxing and UFC have weight classes to ensure fair fights. This is a range of weights the fighters must weiigh in at to compete.
Rounds
Boxing and UFC have rounds to ensure the fighters have time to rest and recover. Boxing has 12 rounds while UFC has 3-5 rounds depending on the fight.
Fighting Style
Boxing is a stand up sport while UFC is a mixed martial art sport.
Mixed Martial Arts
UFC is a mixed martial art sport that allows for a variety of fighting styles including striking, grappling, and submissions. Also reffered to as MMA.

Combat sports are where I feel G.O.A.T. debates are more practical. Since these sports are solo competitive sports, this leads to a more direct comparisions. Things like weight classes and periods of competition will be the big divide. The 2 primary sports of boxing and UFC have strong fanbases with the G.O.A.T. debate chnaging names every few years. As competition becomes more exciting and the skills of the fighters grow, the gap between the brutal brawls of the past and the technical beatdowns of today are separating the fighters. Nowadays the fighters Manny Pacquiao, Floyd Mayweather Jr., Muhammad Ali, Sugar Ray Robinson take over the boxing conversation. There are fighter who are making waves and wanting that title like TerranceBud Crawford, Gervonte Tank Davis, and Canelo Alveraz

In the UFC the G.O.A.T. debate is much more recent since the sport itself is much younger. The names that come up are Jon Jones, Georges St-Pierre, Anderson Silva, Khabib Nurmagomedov, and Demetrious Johnson. The sport is still young and the names will continue to change as the years go on. There is talks about who cheated and who was not cheating however the dominance of the fighters stated cannot be overlooked. Every fighter has right to the title.

G.W.O.A.T

The G.W.O.A.T. debate is much more recent and not as widely discussed. That is not to say there are not some great atheletes who have made a strong case for the title. The most common names that come up are Serena Williams, Amanda Nunes, Ronda Rousey, Diana Taurasi, Mia Hamm, and Simone Biles. The list can go on for days as each one of these atheletes can be regarded as the best in their respective sports.
